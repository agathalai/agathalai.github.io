Assign Identified Geochemical Risks to Mine Facilities

As the mine plan begins to get developed, the results from the previous stages need to be integrated into the mine plan. The GCMsee "geochemical conceptual model" changes from general weathering of rock to reactivity of the rock types in their final storage conditions and configuration.

Typical facilities (the list will need to be site specific) that need to be considered include:

  • Waste rock storage facilities
  • Tailings storage facility
  • Open pits â€“ need to consider exposed rock walls and benches
  • Underground sump water
  • Surface ore stockpiles
  • Borrow source material (if different from waste rock)
  • Dry stack tailings facilities
  • Heap leach facilities
  • Water treatment sludge

It may turn out that much of the waste rock has a low potential for ML/ARDmetal leaching & acid rock drainage and therefore a suitable source of material for construction. On another hand, portions of PAGsee "potentially ARD generating" rock or tailings may need separate management plans and could possibly be considered for re-handling to be used as backfill underground or in minded out pits. There are many other potential options, but the discussion needs to begin as soon as possible during project development.

If a borrow source is needed for construction purposes, it should be treated like mine rock to determine if it can be a source of ML/ARDmetal leaching & acid rock drainage. Testing should start as in the Initial Exploration Stage.

Waste rock can be utilized as construction material if test work has been completed to demonstrate that it is has a low ML/ARDmetal leaching & acid rock drainage potential. However, additional regulatory testing and approval may be needed before mine rock can be used as borrow material.
