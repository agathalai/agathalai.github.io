ML/ARD Block Modeling

An ML/ARDmetal leaching & acid rock drainage block model for waste management can be an incredibly powerful tool and a ‘game changer’ for assessing environmental risk and therefore be a key input to mine design and operations. In the same way a gold block model is developed, so often can an ML/ARDmetal leaching & acid rock drainage block model be developed if the necessary information is available (i.e., as described in this guide). The output of a model will be an overall ML/ARDmetal leaching & acid rock drainage estimation including the number of PAGsee "potentially ARD generating" or non-PAGsee "potentially ARD generating" blocks (or if metal enrichment is being investigated, blocks of metal enrichment). Timing for the development of a ML/ARDmetal leaching & acid rock drainage block model is best started after the ore block model has been established and in discussion with the project geostatician in order to ensure the necessary information is collected. General guidance on data requirements includes the following:

  • Multi-element analyses completed on drill core samples.
  • Determination of sulphur forms (e.g., sulphide, sulphate) and the correlation between multi-element sulphur and sulphide sulphurThe sulphur attributed to the sulphide content of a sample.. For example, sulphur from aqua regiaA mixture of nitric acid (HNO3) and hydrochloric acid (HCl) in a molar ratio of 1:3. This digestion will not fully dissolve silicate minerals. Aqua regia digests are suitable for dissolution of carbonate, oxide and certain sulfides, and provides a semi-quantitative dissolution of precious and base metals. digest and multi-element scans will typically be significantly correlated with sulphide sulphurThe sulphur attributed to the sulphide content of a sample., but it will often require a regression correction.
  • Determination of effective carbonate content and correlation to multi-element results. Often a multi-element regression is required between carbonate content and calcium, magnesium, potassium and sodium in order to remove the influence of calcium and magnesium silicates on carbonate content.

Over time, it is may be possible for more stepwise or recipe-like procedure to be developed for ARDsee "acid rock drainage" block modelling. However, at this time, ARDsee "acid rock drainage" block modelling should be performed in consultation with geochemical specialists and the geology team.
