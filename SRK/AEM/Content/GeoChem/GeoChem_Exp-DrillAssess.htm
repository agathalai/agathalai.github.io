Exploration Drilling Assessment

At the onset of any exploration drilling program, a discussion between the geologists and geochemists (and likely other groups such as metallurgy) should take place to maximize available data from drilling efforts. For example, the geologists logging the core in some instances may not realize that getting a visual estimate of carbonate content can be very helpful to early-stage ARDsee "acid rock drainage" assessment for a project.

A template has been developed to capture recommended information that may exist for exploration programs and accompanies this guide. For more information, see Geochemical Data Interpretation Template and the two specific sheets are located under “Stage 1”.

Drill core information to be compiled and assessed in the template includes the following:

Future versions of this guide may include substantial changes to the type and amount of information obtained from drill core assessments. Research is on-going for automated instrumentation to collect physical and chemical information continuously. These advancements would be very beneficial for economic resource development, but also for all other project development studies including geochemical risk identification and assessment.
