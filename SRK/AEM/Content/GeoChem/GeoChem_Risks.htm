Geochemical Risks

There are many examples in the mining industry where unidentified geochemical risks manifested into detrimental water quality that required expensive and unsustainable management. Two such examples are closed sites within Agnico Eagle’s portfolio and include the Cobalt mining area and the Eagle/Telbel mines (often referred as Joutel). These properties still require technical and financial resources on an annual basis despite Agnico Eagle not having extracted ore from these sites in decades.

For Agnico Eagle to minimize geochemical risks and operate its mine sites in the most sustainable way possible, early identification and assessment of potential risks associated to metal leachingThe process of solid material containing metals and other elements being dissolved into an aqueous solution. and acid rock drainageThe acidic water that is generated when sulphide minerals are exposed to air and water producing sulphuric acid. (ML/ARDmetal leaching & acid rock drainage) is needed. Every stage of project development should consider geochemical risk identification and management from the moment a geologist starts considering a land package as a potential ore deposit through to operation and post-closure. Geochemical risk management ultimately needs to be a part of broader planning for a project and in unison with geology, metallurgy, and mining teams as it is a key input to environmental design and engineering of a project.

Geochemical risk identification is no different than geotechnical studies where the sooner information is obtained, the better.
