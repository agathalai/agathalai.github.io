Data Interpretation

At the completion of each drilling campaign, the multi-element data should be plotted with existing data (as described in Initial Exploration Stage) to ensure that the samples submitted for acid-base accountingA screening procedure whereby the balance between the acid-neutralizing potential (NP) and the acid-generating potential (AP) of rock samples is determined. and mineralogy characterization still capture the range of variability. If more than 10% of new samples plot outside established variability, then these samples should also be submitted for detailed geochemical testing outlined in Analytical Testing Guidance.

Standard practice for estimating ARDsee "acid rock drainage" potential requires first establishing the amount of sulphide in a sample that will contribute to acid potential (APsee "acid-generating potential"), and then the more conservative form neutralization potential (NPsee "neutralizing potential"). The ratio between NPsee "neutralizing potential" and APsee "acid-generating potential" (hereafter referred to as the neutralization potential ratio, or NPR) determines whether a sample is potentially ARD generatingA rock sample that is likely to generate acidity over a certain time period once it is exposed to atmospheric oxygen and water. (PAGsee "potentially ARD generating"), non-PAGsee "potentially ARD generating", or uncertain. When the type of carbonate is not known (e.g., calcite, dolomite, siderite, ankerite, etc.), best practice is to assume samples with an NPR greater than 3 are non-PAGsee "potentially ARD generating", less than 1 are PAGsee "potentially ARD generating" and between 1 and 3 are uncertain. As the carbonate form is better understood, the uncertain category can typically be reduced to between 1 and 2 if the carbonate is predominantly calcite and dolomite.

For waste management planning, typically uncertain material is classified as PAGsee "potentially ARD generating". For example, 10% PAGsee "potentially ARD generating", 5% uncertain and 85% non-PAGsee "potentially ARD generating" would typically be managed as 15% PAGsee "potentially ARD generating" and 85% non-PAGsee "potentially ARD generating".

This is because the small amount of acid produced can be consumed by other components of the rock mass other than carbonates.
