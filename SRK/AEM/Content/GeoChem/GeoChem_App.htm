Application

The goal of this guide is to provide Agnico Eagle with an industry leading geochemical management approach to reduce operations and closure costs while lowering environmental risk and ensuring long-term sustainability of our operations at the lowest cost possible.

This guide can be used for metal leachingThe process of solid material containing metals and other elements being dissolved into an aqueous solution. and acid rock drainageThe acidic water that is generated when sulphide minerals are exposed to air and water producing sulphuric acid. (ML/ARDmetal leaching & acid rock drainage) risk identification for mining projects under development or consideration for acquisition by Agnico Eagle, including greenfield and brownfield projects. This guide can also be used to assess the completeness of the work done by consulting teams when the work needs to be completed by people external to Agnico Eagle.

The focus of this guide is on the internal needs of Agnico Eagle to identify and manage geochemical risks and it is linked with the Responsible Mining Management System (RMMS). While the data obtained by using this guide would likely support any permit application, testing has not been designed to comprehensively satisfy permitting requirements.

Use of this guide is based primarily on project stages: initial exploration, scoping study, feasibility study, and operation and closure. Additional considerations include activities and costs, scheduling, geological setting, data quality, and project evaluation. This guide should be used as follows:

  1. Identify project stage.
  2. Prepare an inventory of available data.
  3. Develop test program based on guidance.
  4. Review additional geological considerations and add any necessary tests.
  5. Consult with Agnico Eagleâ€™s geochemistry specialists to confirm approach.
  6. Execute program.

Testing in this guide was designed to build upon itself through each stage of mine project development and leverages the effort, cost, and information obtained during drilling activities.

This is relatively straightforward to apply to projects that Agnico Eagle develops from discovery, but less so for projects that Agnico Eagle acquires at later stages of mine engineering design. This is because industry standards for geochemical characterization are not always applied.

As a result, if the existing geochemical characterization program for an acquisition project does not meet to the guidance provided herein, then earlier stage testing requirements will need to be completed. Considerations for time allocation in project management schedules are provided in Activities and Costs.

Project evaluation teams should also use this guide given that some testing typically occurs to confirm gold grades and extraction efficiency, and environmental risks before completing an acquisition. The testing recommendations provided in this guide can be used as a checklist for data requests from potential project vendors and a summary for project evaluation teams has been provided in Project Evaluation. When requested data is not available, it should be considered an acquisition risk. Future versions of this guide will provide a risk matrix following the HSEC Risk Analysis of AEM, but at present, the geochemistry specialists should be contacted to discuss level of significance of identified risks.

Activities and Costs gives the general objectives of this guide and how its content is organized. It should be reviewed prior to initiating any program design. Additional references are also provided for any team wanting further explanations on the testing approach.
