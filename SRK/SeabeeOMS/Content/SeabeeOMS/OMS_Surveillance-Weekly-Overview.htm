Overview

Objectives

Comprehensive visual surveillance, performed weekly and at other designated intervals, is critical to all dams and dikes and is conducted to discern potential or developing problems.

Scope

Comprehensive weekly inspections of the TMFs include the following necessary components and features:

Component

Features

Tailings Deposition and Water Management Infrastructure
  • Tailings deposition pipelines
  • Reclaim lines
  • Water lines
  • Pump-back lines
  • All pipeline connections
  • Pumps (floating, diversion, and backup pumps)
  • Discharge locations

East Lake TMF
  • Rockfill Expansion Dike
  • Concrete Dams (1, 2A, 2B, 2C, and 3)
  • Tailings deposition basin
  • Seepage Ponds (1, 2, and 3)
  • Back Pond

\Triangle Lake TMF
  • North Dam
  • South Dam
  • Portage North Dam
  • Tailings deposition basin
  • North Dam Seepage Collection System

Instructions for inspections of specific structures and features are outlined in in the additional instructions below. Once a deficiency has been identified, the prescribed actions should be implemented as soon as practicably possible.

Frequency

Regular comprehensive inspections are performed weekly unless directed otherwise. They are also performed by the environmental manager in April (or immediately after spring thaw) and September, as well as following any significant seismic or climatic event.

Responsible Persons

Visual surveillance is to be undertaken by environmental personnel experienced in discerning potential or developing problems through visual inspection.
