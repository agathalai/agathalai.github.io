East Lake TMF Seepage Collection Ponds

  • The following tables provide reporting, immediate actions, and detailed instructions for overtopping conditions at the EL TMF Seepage Collection Ponds.
  • Corrective Action Conditions (Orange) are associated with the EPP.
  • Critical Condition (Red) area associated with the ERP.

Green

Acceptable Condition

Reporting

None

Action

Continue operation, maintenance, and surveillance guidelines as specified in corresponding sections of this manual.

Orange

Corrective Action Condition

Reporting

Implement TARP Reporting Procedure (Corrective Action - Appendix D).

Immediate Actions

Lower water level.

Detailed Instructions
  • Commence operation of the standby pumps to discharge excess water into the EL TMF Basin.
  • Start the Water Treatment Plant (if not already running). Consider evaporator, discuss with EOR.
  • Decrease or stop tailings deposition in the Back Pond to reduce seepage to the Pond 4 Wet Well.
  • Decrease freshwater usage in the camp, which may increase the amount of reclaim to the mill.

Red

Critical Condition

Reporting

Implement TARP Reporting Procedure (Critical Condition - Appendix D).

Immediate Actions
  • Block off and restrict access to the Collection Pond Wet Wells and surrounding area.
  • Focus all efforts on outreach, planning, and preparation until the overtopping event has concluded and conditions at the area of influence are stable.
  • Implement associated tasks (as applicable) once it is safe to do so.

Detailed Instructions
  • Stop tailings deposition in the Back Pond.
  • Lower any remaining pond in the facility if possible following Tasks outlined in the EPP (Corrective Action Condition above).
  • Arrange immediate site visit with the EOR.
  • If Laonil Lake is impacted: distribute notice to stop use of freshwater in camp.
  • Repair any structural damage in accordance with EOR recommendations
  • Conduct comprehensive study of the impact of the release on the downstream environment/waterbody.
  • Based on conclusions/recommendations of all post-impact studies, work with the EOR to develop a remediation plan.

Triangle Lake TMF Phase 1 – November 2024 to July 2027

  • The following tables provide reporting, immediate actions, and detailed instructions for overtopping conditions at TL TMF Phase 1 from November 2024 until Phase 1 and 2 are combined in July 2027.
  • Corrective Action Conditions (Orange) are associated with the EPP.
  • Critical Condition (Red) area associated with the ERP, see Phase 2 Section 12.6.4. For a Red condition to occur, both basins would become flooded.

Green

Acceptable Condition

Reporting

None

Action

Continue operation, maintenance, and surveillance guidelines as specified in corresponding sections of this manual.

Yellow

Early Warning Condition

Reporting

Implement EPRP reporting procedure in (Early Warning - Appendix D).

Action

Increase frequency of monitoring, surveillance, and data collection as per the TARP (Appendix B).

Orange 1

Corrective Action Condition

Reporting

Implement TARP Reporting Procedure (Corrective Action - Appendix D).

Immediate Actions

Lower pond level.

Detailed Instructions
  • Start the Water Treatment Plant (if not already running). Consider evaporator, discuss with EOR.
  • Discharge water into TL TMF Phase 2 if deemed necessary by the EOR.
  • Decrease or stop the transfer of water from EL TMF into the TL TMF Phase 1 basin if recommended by the EOR.
  • Stop the transfer of water from TL TMF Phase 2 basin to TL TMF Phase 1 basin.
  • Decrease freshwater usage in camp.

Orange 2

Corrective Action Condition

Reporting

Implement TARP Reporting Procedure (Corrective Action - Appendix D).

Immediate Actions
  • Considered loss of control in Phase 1 Basin.
  • *Evacuate personnel from South Dam and block off and restrict access to South Dam.

Detailed Instructions
  • Continue all items in Orange 1 Corrective Action that don’t require personnel to access the South Dam.
  • Once the South Dam is blocked off, allow South Dam to overtop into the Phase 2 Basin.
  • Place staged turbidity barriers at the outlet of Laonil Lake and Pine Lake if deemed necessary by the EOR.
  • Arrange immediate site visit with the EOR.

Red

Critical Condition

Reporting

Implement TARP Reporting Procedure (Critical Condition - Appendix D).

Immediate Actions
  • Block off and restrict access to the TL TMF. Specifically, travel across North Dam.
  • Focus all efforts on outreach, planning, and preparation until overtopping has concluded and conditions at the area of influence are stable and there is input from the EOR.
  • Implement associated tasks (as applicable) once it is safe to do so.

Detailed Instructions
  • Stop tailings deposition into the TL TMF.
  • If possible, lower any remaining supernatant ponding in the facility according to the sequence of tasks outlined in the EPP.
  • Arrange follow up site visit with the EOR.
  • Activate Crisis Management Team/Plan.
  • Repair any structural damage in accordance with EOR recommendations
  • If Laonil Lake is impacted: distribute notice to stop use of freshwater in camp.
  • Conduct comprehensive study of the impact of the release on the downstream environment/waterbody.
  • Based on the conclusions/recommendations of all post-impact studies, work with the EOR to develop a remediation plan.
