East Lake TMF Seepage Collection Ponds

The following tables provide reporting, immediate actions, and detailed instructions for overtopping conditions at the East Lake TMF Seepage Collection Ponds.

Green

Acceptable Condition

Reporting

None

Action

Continue operation, maintenance, and surveillance guidelines as specified in corresponding sections of this manual.

Yellow

Early Warning Condition

Reporting

Implement EPRP reporting procedure in (Early Warning - Appendix D).

Action

Increase frequency of monitoring, surveillance, and data collection as per the TARP (Appendix B)

Orange

Corrective Action Condition

Reporting

Implement TARP Reporting Procedure (Corrective Action - Appendix D).

Immediate Actions

Lower water level.

Detailed Instructions
  • Discharge excess water into the East Lake TMF basin, which is then pumped to the Triangle Lake TMF.
  • Discharge excess water to the Back Pond.
  • Stop the transfer pump from Triangle Lake TMF to the Back Pond so reclaim can draw down the Back Pond.
  • Start the water treatment plant (if not already running) and the evaporators.
  • Decrease or stop tailings deposition in the East Lake TMF.
  • Decrease freshwater usage in the camp, which may increase the amount of reclaim to the mill.
  • At last resort, discharge to the environment. This would be done in a controlled manner but would be a release of supernatant to the environment.

Red

Critical Condition

Reporting

Implement TARP Reporting Procedure (Critical Condition – Appendix D).

Immediate Actions
  • Block off and restrict access to the affected area.
  • Focus all efforts on outreach, planning, and preparation until the failure event has concluded and conditions at the area of influence are stable.
  • Implement associated (as applicable) once it is safe to do so.

Detailed Instructions
  • Lower any remaining pond in the facility if possible following Tasks outlined in the EPP.
  • Arrange immediate site visit with the EOR.
  • If Laonil Lake is impacted: distribute notice to stop use of freshwater in camp.
  • Repair any structural damage in accordance with EOR recommendations
  • Conduct comprehensive study of the impact of the release on the downstream environment/waterbody.
  • Work with the EOR to develop plan for remedial action, based on conclusions of all post-impact studies completed.
