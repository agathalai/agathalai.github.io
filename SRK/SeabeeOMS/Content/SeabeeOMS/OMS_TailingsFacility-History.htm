General History of the Tailings Facilities

The East Lake TMF is the older of the two TMFs and was originally a natural lake that was converted to a TMF when Seabee Mine was initially developed. The natural lake basin provided containment from about 1991 to 2002.

In 2001, an environmental impact statement (EIS) was completed for a TMF expansion at Seabee (KHS 2001), which included raising the East Lake TMF and development of the Triangle Lake TMF. The East Lake TMF expansion included construction of three concrete containment structures, approximately 2 m in height, which provided additional tailings storage up until 2004 (Clifton 2004b). At this time, tailings deposition was relocated to the Triangle Lake TMF, which was also an historical lake converted to a TMF. To provide the required storage capacity, two concrete containment structures, the North Dam and South Dam, were constructed to a crest elevation at 454 m at the north and south sides of the TMF, respectively (Clifton 2003a, Clifton 2003b, Clifton 2004a, Clifton 2006a).

Additional reserves at Seabee Mine and surrounding areas (Santoy 7, Santoy 8, and Santoy Gap) were discovered in the interim, and as a result the mine required another increase in tailings storage capacity. In 2009, an EIS was completed for the Santoy 8 Satellite Mine that included details regarding the expansion of the Triangle Lake TMF (Clifton 2006b). The tailings expansion included raising the containment structures at the Triangle Lake TMF to crest elevation 460.1 m, which would provide an additional five years of storage at a production rate of 1,100 tpd (Golder 2008). This storage capacity was later deemed insufficient and in 2012, an expansion design for the East Lake TMF was developed (SRK 2012). This expansion included building a 6 to 8 m high, 900 m long permeable waste rock dike (utilizing staged construction) within the existing TMF footprint to manage the supernatant pond and seepage. The expansion provided an additional 850,000 m3 of tailings capacity and the combined TMFs accommodated tailings storage to approximately spring 2020 at a production rate of 1,100 tpd.

At the end of 2016, the Triangle Lake TMF was constructed to its final permitted elevation (at that time) of 460.1 m and Stage 1 expansion of the East Lake TMF was completed to elevation 463 m.

Capacity of the TMFs at Seabee have been increased recently. The Phase 1, Triangle Lake TMF expansion to permitted crest elevation of 463.0 m was completed throughout 2019 and 2020 and included raising the North Dam from 460.1 to 466 m, raising the South Dam from 460.1 to 463 m, and construction of the Portage North Dam to 466 m (SRK 2020b). Further to the Triangle Lake TMF expansion, Stage 2 and 3 raises were constructed at the East Lake TMF, raising the crest elevation to 465.0 m by the end of 2019 (SRK 2019e). Expansion of the Triangle Lake TMF will provide an additional 3.1 Mm3 of tailings capacity (10 years of tailings capacity at 1,200 tpd). The combined TMFs will provide storage to approximately spring 2030.
