Triagnle Lake TMF: North Dam

The North Dam is located at the north end of the Triangle Lake TMF. Methods of construction varied with time.

The initial construction of the North Dam consisted of reinforced concrete founded on a prepared bedrock foundation that included a grout curtain to elevation 452.13 m. From this elevation to 460.1 m a grout curtain was not required (Golder 2007).

The low permeability component of the North Dam initially consisted of reinforced concrete lined with HDPE to elevation 452.13 m, followed by a bituminous liner from 452.13 to 454.0 mm and an 80 mm thick HDPE geomembrane from 454.0 m to 466.0 m. Later expansion stages included a modified design that no longer utilized concrete.

Prior to 2019, the raises to the North Dam were done using mine rock, crushed rock bedding layers, and a low-permeability liner. The bedrock foundation along new sections of the dam was prepared with a bentonite-amended granular mixture (Golder 2007 and Golder 2010b).

Expansion of the North Dam in 2019 and 2020 included an HDPE liner secured to reinforced concrete plinths constructed along the prepared bedrock foundation between 460.1 and 466.0 m.

Bulk dam fill in the most recent expansion comprised blasted run of quarry rock with crushed rock bedding layers placed on either side of the HDPE liner (SRK 2020b).

Triangle Lake TMF: North Seepage Collection System

The seepage collection system was designed to collect and manage surficial seepage that was observed along the downstream toe of the structure. Construction included the following steps:

  1. Excavation of a drainage channel in bedrock.
  2. Construction of a French drain (comprising nonwoven geotextile, drain rock, perforated drainpipe with thermistor cable and riser pipes).
  3. Placement of backfill above the French drain.
  4. Installation of a heated sump.
  5. Installation of a pump and discharge line for continual pump-back of seepage to the Triangle Lake TMF.

The seepage collection system consists of East and West Collection Trenches bisected by the heated sump (Figure 6).

During the 2019 Phase 1 expansion of the Triangle Lake TMF, access into the sump was maintained by constructing a cast-in-place concrete structure above the sump pit and then placing a concrete cylindrical manhole sections within the dam fill to the 453 m downstream bench level (SRK 2020b).

Triangle Lake TMF: Saddle Dikes W2 and W2A

Saddle dikes W2 and W2A were constructed in 2010 at bedrock lows adjacent to and east of the North Dam (crest elevation 460.1 m). They were incorporated in the North Dam structure when this dam was raised to 466 m as part the Phase 1 expansion of the Triangle Lake TMF. Triangle Lake TMF: South Dam

The South Dam is located at the south end of the Phase 1 Triangle Lake TMF.

The construction of South Dam is summarized as follows:

  • Consolidation grouting was completed along the bedrock foundation of the initial length of South Dam (approximately 325 m).
  • The low permeability component of the South Dam initially consisted of reinforced concrete lined with 80 mm thick HDPE geomembrane to elevation 454.0 m. The HDPE liner forms the impermeable element from 454.0 to 463.0 m (current and final permitted height of the structure).
  • The HDPE liner was secured to reinforced concrete plinths constructed along the upstream toe between 460.1 and 463.0 m in 2019. Crushed rock bedding layers were placed directly beneath the HDPE liner to separate it from the bulk dam fill material that consisted of blasted run-of-quarry rock (SRK 2020b).
