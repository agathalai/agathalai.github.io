Objectives

The objectives of the daily inspections are to:

  • identify any breakage/leakage within the pipelines;
  • identify potential pump failure;
  • inspect seepage collection trench performance; and
  • inspect the general condition of the tailings surface and deposition points.

Scope

Routine daily inspections include visual inspection of the general condition of tailings dams and the catchment basins, such as tailings discharge and surface water ponding, and in specifically, the conditions of the following elements:

  • tailings pipelines;
  • reclaim pipelines;
  • booster station;
  • all required pumps, EL TMF seepage collection pond wet wells; and
  • operation of the Triangle Lake North Dam seepage collection system.

Frequency

Inspections should take place twice per 12-hour shift (at least) unless otherwise directed.

Responsibility

All those involved with operation of the tailings facilities should be continuously diligent in observing for unusual or problematic signs of TMF performance whilst performing their daily procedures. This includes such things as the general condition of tailings dams/dikes and the basins. Responsible positions include the mill operating personnel and site services personnel.

The chain of command that must be followed when a deficiency is identified during a daily inspection is provided in Appendix A.

Documentation

The surveillance process relies on the proper documentation of daily inspections and of the observation of any noted deficiencies on the relevant forms. These include inspection and sign-off sheets for the mill-reclaim pump shacks and site services (i.e., TL TMF sump or tailings and reclaim, as applicable).

Current inspections forms are up to date and in accordance with this manual. Inspection forms are found in Appendix A.
