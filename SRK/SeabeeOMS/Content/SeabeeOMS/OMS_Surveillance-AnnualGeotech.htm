Annual Geotechnical Inspection

Objectives

The objectives of the annual geotechnical inspection are to

  • gain an external review of the facilities operation and performance by the EOR and
  • comply with the Seabee and Santoy 8 Mines Approval to Operate Pollutant Control Facilities issued by the Saskatchewan Ministry of Environment (SK MOE Approval No. PO19-193, Clause 4.3).

Scope

The annual geotechnical inspection consists of an external inspection of the facilities operation and performance. This inspection must be carried out in summer/fall and should be based on inspection of the structures summarized in Section 6.5.2.

Frequency

An annual geotechnical inspection is to be carried out once per year during the late spring to summer months when all aspects of each facility are easily accessible and visible.

Responsible Persons

An annual geotechnical inspection is to be carried out by the EOR or a qualified third-party geotechnical engineer.

Documentation

This inspection must be documented in a detailed factual report that must include the following:

  • Findings and recommendations on the facilities' performance considering the inspector’s observations, interviews with mine operations staff responsible for the operation of the TMFs, and a review and analysis of all detailed monitoring data for the facility.
  • The reviewer must generate a photographic account of the inspections and provide any needed recommendations with respect to remedial actions.

The report must be delivered in a timely manner so that, if required, mitigation measures can be carried out to address any areas of concern regarding the facilities.

The latest annual geotechnical inspection was completed by SRK in 2019 (SRK 2020a).

Guidance

The annual geotechnical review must comply with the Saskatchewan Ministry of Environment’s Approval to Operate Pollutant Control Facilities, SK MOE Approval No. PO19-193, issued for the Seabee and Santoy 8 Mines.
