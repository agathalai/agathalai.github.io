Geochemical Properties of Tailings

SGO has characterized tailings for the potential of metal leaching and acid rock drainage (ML/ARD) since 2013 with the results reported to SMOE as part of their annual reporting commitments. Available geochemical tests used to assess the ML/ARD potential of tailings material includes static analysis by acid-base accounting (ABA) and trace elements from the operational monitoring of mill tailings which was initiated in 2013 (SRK 2013 to 2017, SGO 2018 to 2020), and historic mill and in situ tailings sampling programs dating back to 2000 (SRK 2012b, SRK 2013). Kinetic testing with laboratory based humidity cells and a field barrel test were also initiated in 2021. These tests are used to assess the long-term behaviour and leachate characteristics as oxidation of tailings progresses.

Tailings monitoring in the mill indicates the temporal evolution of the geochemical characteristics of tailings placed in the TMFs. Before 2014, tailings were produced from 2-B ore only and from 2004 to August 2018, 2-B ore was blended with Santoy ore. Since 2019, tailings have been produced from Santoy 8 ore only. Overall, sulphide content of tailings produced from milling has low sulphide content (<0.5%). In general, ore from the 2-B mine have higher sulphide content in comparison to ore from the Santoy deposits, with the tailings produced from the 2-B ore also having higher sulphide content relative to Santoy tailings. A summary of tailings deposition over time is as follows:

  • 2-B Mine tailings were deposited at the EL TMF from 1991 to end of 2003;

  • 2-B and Santoy 8 Mine tailings were blended and deposited in TL TMF from 2004 to end of April 2016;

  • 2-B and Santoy 8 Mine tailings were blended and deposited in EL TMF from May 2015 to October 2016;

  • 2-B and Santoy 8 Mine tailings were blended and deposited in TL TMF from November 2016 to April 2017 (note, at this time production was predominately from the Santoy mine);

  • 2-B and Santoy 8 Mine tailings were blended and deposited in EL TMF from May 2017 to October 2018 (as of August 2018, the tailings were solely from processing Santoy ore);

  • Santoy 8 Mine tailings were deposited in TL TMF from November 2018 to April 2019;

  • Santoy 8 Mine tailings were deposited in EL TMF from May to October 2019 (subsequently, the EL TMF was put into care & maintenance); and

  • Santoy 8 Mine tailings have been deposited in the TL TMF since November 2019 and will continue until June 2030. Tailings will be placed in the EL TMF (current Back Pond area) for approximately six months in 2023 to support the EL TMF stabilization works (SRK 2022).

Results show a range of ARD classification ranging from potentially acid generating (PAG) to non-PAG.

Ongoing and Further Studies

In 2020, a geochemical characterization program was initiated and samples were collected from the EL and TL TMFs with the purpose of refining the geochemical understanding of tailings characteristics and long-term behaviour through kinetic testing. The program is separated into two phases, with Phase 1 including static geochemical assessment. The objectives of the Phase 1 testing include:

  • Assessment of the geochemical variability of surface tailings at each of the EL and TL TMFs;

  • Assessment of potential geochemical variability in grain size from hydraulic sorting on exposed beaches; and

  • Characterization of future ore sampled from drill core as a surrogate for future tailings to assess if geochemical characteristics of future tailings are expected to change.

Phase 2 of the characterization program includes assessment of leachate characteristics through kinetic testing including humidity cell tests and field barrel tests. The objectives of Phase 2 include:

  • Assess non-acidic ML rates;

  • Develop a site-specific criterion for the classification of ARD potential of tailings;

  • Determine if acidic conditions are expected to form and timing to onset; and

  • Initiate field barrel test program to assess weathering rates and leachate characteristics for the site climatic conditions.

Over 30 years of seepage monitoring of the TMFs has shown neutral pH conditions being maintained; however, further understanding of tailings characteristics are needed to determine if overall neutral pH conditions are expected to persist. However, the ongoing kinetic testing program has shown development of acidic conditions from two samples of tailings materials collected from near surface materials which are classified as PAG indicating potential for localized development of acidic conditions within the EL and TL TMFs. The kinetic test program is ongoing, and with results used to assess the effectiveness of neutralization potential provided by silicate dissolution which may be used to refine the development of site-specific criteria for defining ARD classification.

In early 2024, a drill program was completed in the EL TMF with geochemical investigations completed to address gaps in understanding of the geochemical characteristics and expected weathering behaviour of the tailings materials.

This included:

  • Additional sampling through the entire tailings profile at the EL TMF capturing the full history of material deposition,
  • Collection of pore water samples to characterize in situ tailings porewater,
  • Determine the depth of the phreatic surface to assess the proportion of subaerial tailings materials, and
  • Installation of pore gas monitoring wells to assess the depth of penetration of oxygen ingress within the tailings profile.

The results from this program will be used to assess the characteristics of tailings throughout the depositional history, provide water quality data from pore water samples for development of source term water quality predictions, and assess the depth of reactive tailings. Results from this program are pending.

The results will be used to support development of closure plans for the TMFs which consider the geochemical stability of tailings materials for the next D&R update in 2026.
