The current configuration (Stage 3) of the EL TMF is shown in Figure 3. It includes three concrete dams (1, 2, and 3) that are used as seepage cut-off walls for the purposes of directing seepage. The maximum tailings storage elevation of the EL TMF Stage 3 Expansion is 464.5 m, which includes a tailings (not water) freeboard of 0.5 m. The history of tailings deposition and dam/dike construction is summarized below.

Year

Milestone

1991
  • Regulatory approval to dewater the EL basin and use it to deposit mill effluent and tailings.
  • The natural basin of EL provided containment for the first six years of operation.

1997
  • Construction of a 0.5 m high concrete dam across a topographic low, blocking the drainage leading to Laonil Lake.

1998
  • Construction of three rockfill dams lined with 60 mm HDPE at the northeast (Dam 1), southeast (Dam 2) and southwest (Dam 3) corners of EL.
  • The dams were initially built to a crest elevation at 457.5 m.

2000

Raising of the three EL dams to a crest elevation of 458.5 m.

2002
  • Detection and investigation of elevated concentrations of cyanide in Laonil Lake and piezometers downstream of Dam 2.
  • Completion of remedial design.
  • Regulatory approval to complete the work in 2003.

2003

Undertaking of remedial work including the following:

  • Replacing sections of Dam 2 with concrete retaining walls.
  • Repairing the seal between the HDPE liner and grout curtain at Dam 2.
  • Replacing Dam 1 and Dam 3 with concrete containment walls (Clifton 2003c and Clifton 2004b).

2004
  • End of tailings deposition in EL TMF.
  • Start of tailings deposition in TL TMF.

2012
  • Design submitted to SK MOE to increase the tailings capacity of EL TMF (SRK 2012).
  • Design concept maintains the crest elevation of Dams 1, 2, and 3 at 460.0 m.
  • Tailings to be raised by constructing an internal rockfill dike.
  • Approval granted to construct a test section of the rockfill dike.

2013
  • Completion of construction of test section (SRK 2014c).
  • Monitoring data from the section used to complete a deformation back-analysis to confirm short- and long-term stability requirements.

2015
  • Submission of a revised design report to SK MOE
  • Approval granted to construct the Stage 1 configuration of the dike: Elev. 463.0 m (SRK 2015f).

2016
  • Completion of Stage 1 construction of the dike (SRK 2016c).

2017
  • Completion of Stage 2 construction.
  • Dike raised from 463.0 to 464.0 masl.

2018
  • Start of Stage 3 raise of dike with most of it raised to elevation 465.0 m.
  • Portion of the downstream crest remained low at elevation 464.0 m.
  • Nonwoven geotextile was installed to a minimum elevation of 465.0 m (SRK 2019e).

2019
  • Completion of the Stage 3 raise to 465.0 m (SRK 2019e).

2020

  • No change to the TMF structures.

  • Static liquefaction concerns and stability analyses associated with the upstream Rockfill Expansion Dike discussed with SGO’s Independent Tailings Review Board (ITRB) in February 2020.

  • The ITRB agreed with the stability assessment findings, the predicted consequences of failure, and the proposed plan for stabilization.

  • Agreed between SGO, SRK, and SGOs ITRB, that stabilization of the TMF was the preferred path forward prior to any additional tailings deposition.

2021

  • No change to the TMF structures.

  • Detailed stabilization design was advanced.

2022
  • Detailed stabilization design was finalized and submitted to the Saskatchewan Ministry of Environment for review in March 2022.

  • Saskatchewan Ministry of Environment approved the stabilization design for construction in May 2022.

  • Construction of the stabilization design planned for completion between May and November 2022.

2023
  • Construction of the stabilization design for the East Arm of the Internal Rockfill Expansion Dike was substantially completed between July and September 2023.

2024
  • Deposition of tailings into Back Pond is scheduled to begin the stabilization of the South Arm of the Internal Rockfill Expansion Dike. Tailings deposition will continue in 2025 to approximately end of July, possibly longer until the designed stabilization configuration is complete.

East Lake TMF: Dam 3

Dam 3 is located at the southwest corner of the EL TMF. Its construction is summarized in the table below.

Parameter

Concrete Dam 3

Foundation

Prepared bedrock consisting of a grout curtain under the legacy concrete wall. Prepared bedrock with slush grout under the new concrete abutments (Figure 2)

Construction
  • Legacy reinforced concrete wall, vertically constructed with 60 mil thick geomembrane (HDPE) installed along the upstream face of the concrete dam limits.
  • Provides tailings and water containment along the southwest corner of EL TMF.
  • Rockfill dam tied into legacy concrete wall.
  • Upstream slope includes 12 oz nonwoven geotextile and 80 mil thick geomembrane (HDPE) for containment.
  • Upstream and downstream slopes are 2.5H:1V and 1.5H:1V, respectively.

East Lake TMF: Rockfill Expansion Dike

The expansion dike is located inside or upstream of concrete dams 1 and 2, and Dam 3. The dike was constructed on previously deposited tailings. Its construction is summarized in the table below. Dike raising is discussed in Dam Raising.

Parameter

Expansion Dike

Foundation

Historical tailings deposited within the TMF.

Construction
  • Perimeter rockfill dike (in general).
  • L-shaped configuration that provides tailings containment along the east and south flanks of the facility.
  • High strength woven geotextile along the interface between the tailings foundation and dike was placed along the south section of the dike to facilitate construction as well as to help control differential settlement.
  • Upstream and downstream slopes are 2.5H:1V and 3H:1V, respectively.
  • Upstream slope includes a 16 oz. nonwoven geotextile that acts as a filter preventing migration of tailings solids through the structure.
  • Designed to retain tailings solids, not water; therefore, seepage will be collected, contained, and managed at four seepage collection areas at the downstream toe of the dike (Figure 2).
  • Concrete Dams 1, 2, and 3 serve as the primary containment for the EL TMF expansion.
  • Although it will not contain water, there is a 0.5 m freeboard for tailings deposition that is required for all dike stages.

Final elevation

466.0 m

Approximate Length

950 m

Crest Width

13.5 m (8 m crest width at final elevation of 466.0 m)

Approximate Height

8 m

East Lake TMF: Rockfill Tailings Retention Berm (RTRB)

The RTRB is located inside the concrete dams 1 and 2, and Dam 3. The northern section of the RTRB was constructed on previously deposited tailings. The southern section of the RTRB was constructed on bedrock. Its construction is summarized in the table below. Dike raising is discussed in Dam Raising.

Parameter

Expansion Dike

Foundation

Historical tailings deposited within the TMF and bedrock.

Construction
  • Perimeter rockfill berm.
  • Provides tailings containment along the southeast flank of the facility.
  • Upstream and downstream slopes are 2H:1V and 3H:1V, respectively.
  • Upstream slope includes a 16 oz. nonwoven geotextile that acts as a filter preventing migration of tailings solids through the structure.
  • Designed to retain tailings solids, not water; therefore, seepage will be collected, contained, and managed at four seepage collection areas at the downstream toe of the dike (Figure 2), specifically at Pond 4 Wet Well.
  • Concrete Dams 1 and 2, and Dam 3 serve as the primary containment for the EL TMF.
  • Although it will not contain water, there is a 0.5 m freeboard for tailings deposition that is required for all dike stages.

Final elevation

465.0 m

Approximate Length

120 m

Crest Width

5 m

Approximate Height

6 m
