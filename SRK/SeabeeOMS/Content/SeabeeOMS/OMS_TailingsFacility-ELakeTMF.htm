The current configuration (Stage 3) of the East Lake TMF is shown in Figure 2 and Figure 5. It includes three concrete dams (1, 2, and 3) that are used as seepage cut-off walls for the purposes of directing seepage. The maximum tailings storage elevation of the East Lake TMF Stage 3 Expansion is 464.5 m, which includes a tailings (not water) freeboard of 0.5 m. The history of tailings deposition and dam/dike construction is summarized below.

Year

Milestone

1991
  • Regulatory approval to dewater the East Lake basin and use it to deposit mill effluent and tailings
  • The natural basin of East Lake provided containment for the first six years of operation

1997
  • Construction of a 0.5 m high concrete dam across a topographic low, blocking the drainage leading to Laonil Lake

1998
  • Construction of three rockfill dams lined with 60 mm HDPE at the northeast (Dam 1), southeast (Dam 2) and southwest (Dam 3) corners of East Lake
  • The dams were initially built to a crest elevation at 457.5 m

2000

Raising of the three East Lake dams to a crest elevation of 458.5 m

2002
  • Detection and investigation of elevated concentrations of cyanide in Laonil Lake and piezometers downstream of Dam 2
  • Completion of remedial design
  • Regulatory approval to complete the work in 2003

2003

Undertaking of remedial work including the following:

  • replacing sections of Dam 2 with concrete retaining walls
  • repairing the seal between the HDPE liner and grout curtain at Dam 2
  • replacing Dam 1 and Dam 3 with concrete containment walls (Clifton 2003c and Clifton 2004b)

2004
  • End of tailings deposition in East Lake TMF
  • Start of tailings deposition in Triangle Lake TMF

2012
  • Design submitted to SK MOE to increase the tailings capacity of East Lake TMF (SRK 2012).
  • Design concept maintains the crest elevation of Dams 1, 2, and 3 at 460.0 m
  • Tailings to be raised by constructing an internal rockfill dike
  • Approval granted to construct a test section of the rockfill dike

2013
  • Completion of construction of test section (SRK 2014c)
  • Monitoring data from the section used to complete a deformation back-analysis to confirm short- and long-term stability requirements

2015
  • Submission of a revised design report to SK MOE
  • Approval granted to construct the Stage 1 configuration of the dike: Elev. 463.0 m (SRK 2015f)

2016
  • Completion of Stage 1 construction of the dike (SRK 2016c)

2017
  • Completion of Stage 2 construction
  • Dike raised from 463.0 to 464.0 masl

2018
  • Start of Stage 3 raise of dike with most of it raised to elevation 465.0 m
  • Portion of the downstream crest remained low at elevation 464.0 m.
  • Nonwoven geotextile was installed to a minimum elevation of 465.0 m (SRK 2019e)

2019
  • Completion of the Stage 3 raise to 465.0 m (SRK 2019e).

East Lake TMF Concrete: Dam 1

The expansion dike is located inside the concrete dams 1, 2, and 3. The dike was constructed on previously deposited tailings. Its construction is summarized in the table below and in Figure 2 and Figure 5. Dike raising is discussed in Dam Raising.

Parameter

Expansion Dike

Foundation

Historical tailings deposited within the TMF

Construction
  • Perimeter rockfill dike (in general)
  • L-shaped configuration that provides tailings containment along the east and south flanks of the facility
  • High strength woven geotextile along the interface between the tailings foundation and dike was placed along the south section of the dike to facilitate construction as well as to help control differential settlement.
  • Upstream and downstream slopes are 2.5H:1V and 3H:1V, respectively
  • Upstream slope includes a 16 oz. nonwoven geotextile that acts as a filter preventing migration of tailings solids through the structure
  • Designed to retain tailings solids, not water; therefore, seepage will be collected, contained, and managed at four seepage collection areas at the downstream toe of the dike (Figure 4 and Figure 5)
  • Concrete Dams 1, 2, and 3 serve as the primary containment for the East Lake TMF expansion
  • Although it will not contain water, there is a 0.5 m freeboard for tailings deposition that is required for all dike stages

Final elevation

466.0 m

Approximate Length

950 m

Width

0.6 m

Approximate Height

8 m

East Lake TMF Concrete: Dam 2

Concrete Dam 2 is located on the east side of the East Lake TMF. It comprises three separate dams (A, B, and C) that are separated by natural bedrock highs (Figure 2). Their construction is summarized in the tables below for Dam 2A and for 2B and 2C.

Concrete Dam 2A

Parameter

Concrete Dam 2A

Foundation

Prepared bedrock consisting of a grout curtain

Construction

Northern Portion

  • Reinforced concrete, vertically constructed
  • North abutment lined with a geomembrane

 

Southern Portion

  • Southern extend comprised of rockfill lined with an HDPE geomembrane liner
  • Southern portion converted to a lined rockfill structure as it was too high to construct a concrete wall to full height (Clifton 2004b and SRK 2015f)
  • Upstream slope of approximately 3.5H:1V
  • Downstream slope that ranged between of 1.7H:1V and 3.5H:1V
  • Toe seal along the southern portion of Dam 2A consists of a surcharged bentonite amended sand mixture between a 60 mm thick HDPE geomembrane and the underlying grout curtain (Clifton 2004b and SRK 2015f)
