Introduction

Objectives

Consistent with the Mining Association of Canada OMS guidelines (MAC 2021b), this manual defines and describes the

  • roles and responsibilities of all personnel assigned to the EL and TL TMFs;
  • procedures and processes for managing change;
  • key components of each facility;
  • procedures required to operate, maintain, and monitor each facility to ensure they perform according to their design, meet regulatory and corporate policy obligations, and link to emergency planning and response; and
  • requirements for analysis and documentation of facility performance.

The organization of this manual was adopted to make it easily consumable as an online (HTML) document. Therefore, the table of contents is further subdivided than what would be typical and features such as table numbering are absent. The OMS should be viewed using the HTML document version when possible.

Scope

This manual

  • applies to the EL and TL TMFs. Note, the Santoy Mine Water Management Facility will be added to the OMS Manual in 2025. This work is already in progress;
  • outlines the management framework that Seabee Gold Operations (SGO) and its contractors are to use to ensure safe operation, maintenance, and surveillance; and
  • provides relevant design, construction, and closure guidance.

Responsibility

The Site Responsible Person at SSR has sole authority over the distribution and revision of this manual and is responsible for providing notifications and training relative to changes in its contents.

SSR may contract with a third party to fulfill any of the above responsibilities.

Procedure for Changes in Design or Operations

The General Manager, Corporate Responsible Person and Site Responsible Person must be notified of any regulatory changes, risk management changes, and changes to the design, construction, operation, or closure plan specified either in the design report or in this manual.
