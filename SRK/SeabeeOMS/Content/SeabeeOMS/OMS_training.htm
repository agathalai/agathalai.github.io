Training and Competence

Overview

The general manager is responsible for ensuring that all staff members associated with tailings deposition and water management are

  • familiar with the contents of this manual;
  • have the appropriate experience;
  • receive appropriate training; and
  • have a clear understanding of their roles and responsibilities.

Staff Competency and Training

SSR Mining will develop a detailed site-specific TMF orientation and training module based on this manual and will require attendance by all relevant staff. This site orientation will inform all site personnel associated with the TMFs with general TMF management principles and stress the need to be continually aware of visual indications of the TMF performance.

All staff need to possess experience and skills in tailings management appropriate for their job description prior to being appointed. Staff have a duty to inform their immediate supervisor of any training requirements they require to fulfill their assigned responsibilities.

On-the-job training for specific tasks is available to staff, such as those outlined in relevant standard operating procedures (SOPs). Staff with insufficient general tailings management experience will be required to complete formal external training, such as online tailings management courses offered by Edumine.

The EOR will conduct an annual assessment of site staff based on this manual (typically during the dam safety inspection). Workshops have been held in Saskatoon in the past and they are planned when there are changes in SGO staff, when there are updates to good practice guidelines or regulations, or to review expert panel reports related to tailings or mine waste failures.

Competency of EOR

At a minimum, the EOR must be a registered professional engineer and be in good standing with the professional association in the jurisdiction of the mine operation. The jurisdiction for SGO is the Association of Professional Engineers and Geoscientists of Saskatchewan (APEGS). As identified in Section 3.1 of the Mining Dams Bulletin (CDA 2019b), for dams with a consequence classification of High or above (Triangle Lake TMF: North Dam), the EOR must have at least 10 years of related experience.
