TMF Containment and Elevations

Both TMFs are constructed as a series of dam structures (Figure 2 and Figure 3) that span between bedrock highs and include the following structures.

  • Laonil Dam

The EL TMF is currently constructed to 465.0 m, 1 m below its ultimate permitted crest elevation of 466.0 m. Beginning in May 2024, tailings deposition transitioned to the EL TMF basin for a duration of two weeks, followed by deposition into Back Pond until November 2024.

The TL TMF has recently undergone (2019 to 2021) a two-phase expansion to accommodate additional tailings volume. The TL TMF is currently segregated into two separate basins, separated by the South Dam, and referred to as the Phase 1 and Phase 2 TL TMFs herein. Active tailings deposition remained in the Phase 1 TL TMF (north of the South Dam) until May 2024. Following mid-November 2024, tailings deposition will transition from the EL TMF to the Phase 2 TL TMF basin to achieve the closure configuration by approximately 2031 (2024-2025 Tailings Deposition Plan and Water Balance Update).

The preliminary tailings deposition and water management plan is provided in the TL TMF Expansion Detailed Design Report (SRK 2015d and SRK 2018b), and the more detailed annual tailings deposition planning between 2021 and early 2024 is provided in Appendix E.

Tailings Deposition and Location

Tailings generated at the Seabee mill are deposited subaerially in either the EL TMF, which is about 2.1 km from the Seabee mill, or TL TMF, which is about 3.1 km from the Seabee mill. Tailings deposition is in accordance with a Saskatchewan Mines Approval to Operate Pollutant Control Facilities (SK MOE Approval No. PO19-193).

Surficial Geology and Physiography

The site is located within the Canadian Shield Physiographic Region. Repeated periods of glaciation produced glacial deposits of poorly sorted glacial till with some sand deposits. The mine site occupies an area of low relief where bedrock outcrops between recessive areas that contain glacial deposits, muskeg, and/or lakes and small ponds (SRK 2012).

Hydrology

Most of the Seabee Mine infrastructure lies within the Laonil Lake drainage basin. The basin measures approximately 28 km2 and is a subset of the larger Churchill River drainage basin (KHS 2001). The latest hydrological assessments that inform the tailings deposition planning water balance are included in SRK (2024h).

East Lake TMF Catchment

The EL TMF (including Back Pond) has a total catchment area of 0.58 km2. A portion of the runoff within the catchment reports to the East Pond through seepage collection ditches and three diversion pumps (SRK 2015f and SRK 2015d). Approximately 0.19 km2 of the catchment area is partially diverted by these three diversion pumps located at the west side of the TMF.

Triangle Lake TMF Catchment

The expanded TL TMF has a primary catchment of about 0.95 km2 (SRK 2018b). This primary catchment is divided between the Phase 1 TL TMF (0.51 km2) and the Phase 2 TL TMF (0.44 km2). The expansion was designed to accommodate runoff without diversions, but could be enhanced with the following constructions:

  • diversion sumps and pumps can be installed on the east side of the TMF; and
  • grading can enhance drainage away from the basin.

Catchment for Combined TMFs

The combined catchment of the EL and TL TMFs, at completion of the TL TMF expansion, will therefore cover approximately 1.5 km2 (150 ha). The current diversion pump configuration at the EL TMF is shown in Figure 2. Diversions previously present (two of them) at the TL TMF were removed following completion of the TL TMF expansion as their location became internal to the expanded facility.

Hydrogeology

Groundwater flow can be characterized by the following:

  • groundwater flow through bedrock is primarily by transport through fractures rather than transport through the impermeable bedrock;
  • groundwater flows away from the EL TMF with moderate gradients toward Pine Lake and Laonil Lake (Neegan Burnside 2001);
  • regional groundwater flows north to north-easterly toward Pine Lake (Neegan Burnside 2001);
  • horizontal groundwater flow velocity for between EL TMF and Laonil Lake is approximately 7.0x107m/s; and
  • horizontal groundwater flow velocity for between TL TMF and Pine Lake: 2.7x107 m/s.

Seismicity

The most recent seismic hazard analysis for the Seabee Mine site was completed by SRK in 2018 (SRK 2018b), in accordance with Canadian Dam Association standards (CDA 2013), and led to the following conclusions:

  • according to the National Building Code Seismic Hazard Calculation, there is a low frequency of occurrence for significant earthquakes in the region;
  • the peak seismic hazard, or PGA, is 0.031 g, with a recurrence interval of 1-in-2,457 years (or a 2% probability of exceedance in 50 years); and
  • SRK will complete a site specific seismic hazard assessment in 2025 for a PGA at 1:10,000 year return period.
