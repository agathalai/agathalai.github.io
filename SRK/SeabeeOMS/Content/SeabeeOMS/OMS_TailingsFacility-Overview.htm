TMF Containment and Elevations

Both TMFs are constructed as a series of dam structures (Figure 2) that span between bedrock highs and include the following structures.

TMF

Components

East Lake TMF
  • Concrete Dams 1, 2, and 3
  • An upstream rockfill Expansion Dike

Triangle Lake TMF
  • North Dam
  • North Dam Seepage Collection System
  • South Dam
  • Portage North Dam

The East Lake TMF is currently constructed to 465.0 m, 1 m below its ultimate permitted crest elevation of 466.0 m.

The Triangle Lake TMF is currently undergoing a two-phase expansion to accommodate additional tailings volume. The preliminary tailings deposition and water management plan is provided in the Triangle Lake TMF Expansion Detailed Design Report (SRK 2015d and SRK 2018b), and the more detailed annual tailings deposition plan for 2020 is provided in SRK 2020c.

Tailings Deposition and Location

Tailings generated at the Seabee mill are deposited subaerially in either the East Lake TMF, which is about 2.1 km from the Seabee mill, or Triangle Lake TMF, which is about 3.1 km from the Seabee mill. Tailings deposition is in accordance with a Saskatchewan Mines Approval to Operate Pollutant Control Facilities (SK MOE Approval No. PO19-193).

Surficial Geology and Physiography

The site is located within the Canadian Shield Physiographic Region. Repeated periods of glaciation produced glacial deposits of poorly sorted glacial till with some sand deposits. The mine site occupies an area of low relief where bedrock outcrops between recessive areas that contain glacial deposits, muskeg, and/or lakes and small ponds (SRK 2012).

Hydrology

Most of the Seabee Mine infrastructure lies within the Laonil Lake drainage basin. The basin measures approximately 28 km2 and is a subset of the larger Churchill River drainage basin (KHS 2001).

East Lake TMF Catchment

The East Lake TMF has a total catchment area of 0.57 km2. A portion of the runoff within the catchment reports to the East Pond through seepage collection ditches and three diversion pumps (SRK 2015f and SRK 2015d). Approximately 0.19 km2 of the catchment area is partially diverted by these three diversion pumps located at the west side of the TMF.

Triangle Lake TMF Catchment

The expanded Triangle Lake TMF will have a primary catchment of about 0.93 km2 (SRK 2018b). The expansion was designed to accommodated runoff without diversions, but can be enhanced with the following constructions:

  • Diversion sumps and pumps can be installed on the east side of the TMF.
  • Grading can enhance drainage away from the basin.

These scenarios are under study as part of the Phase 2 construction.

Hydrogeology

Groundwater flow can be characterized by the following:

  • Groundwater flow through bedrock is primarily by transport through fractures rather than transport through the impermeable bedrock.
  • Groundwater flows away from the East Lake TMF with moderate gradients toward Pine Lake and Laonil Lake (Neegan Burnside 2001).
  • Regional groundwater flows north to northeasterly toward Pine Lake (Neegan Burnside 2001).
  • Horizontal groundwater flow velocity for between East Lake TMF and Laonil Lake is approximately 7.0x107 m/s.
  • Horizontal groundwater flow velocity for between Triangle Lake TMF and Pine Lake: 2.7x107 m/s.

Seismicity

The most recent seismic hazard analysis for the Seabee Mine site was completed by SRK in 2018 (SRK 2018b), in accordance with Canadian Dam Association standards (CDA 2013), and led to the following conclusions:

  • According to the National Building Code Seismic Hazard Calculation, there is a low frequency of occurrence for significant earthquakes in the region.
  • The peak seismic hazard, or peak ground acceleration (PGA), is 0.031 g, with a recurrence interval of 1-in-2,457 years (or a 2% probability of exceedance in 50 years).
