Overview

A water balance for the TMFs was developed in a Microsoft ExcelÂ® spreadsheet to help designated Seabee environment and mill personnel manage the water in the TMFs through to closure. The spreadsheet model and accompanying water level tracking sheet can be updated and altered depending on observations in the field or changes to the water management plan (SRK 2015d).

The water management process diagram should be read in conjunction with the following sections and is provided in Figure 4. Both active and backup pumps and water lines are shown in Figure 4.

Planning and Principles

Maximum Pond Levels

  • Supernatant ponds must not exceed the maximum allowable operating water level of the TMFs. Otherwise, or if within the 0.5 m freeboard, SK MOE must be notified.
  • East Lake TMF is to be operated subaerially and any ponding must be pumped immediately to the Triangle Lake TMF.

Duration of Water Management

  • Water treatment plant must be operated for as long as possible to reduce the water repository at the Triangle Lake TMF (governed by the Water Balance Spreadsheet).
  • Six perimeter diversion sumps and pumps are to operate for as long as possible to reduce inflow to the TMFs.

Mill Water

Current water management needs at the Seabee mill for processing are supplied by the TMFs (95%) and from camp water and freshwater makeup (5%). An upgrade scheduled for 2021 will allow for direct reclaim to the mill from the Triangle Lake TMF.

Location of Ponds

Supernatant ponds are to be maintained at the center of the TMFs (i.e., away from the dams and dikes).

Facilities

East Lake TMF Basin

Maximum allowable water levels in the East Lake TMF tailings basin must be maintained as low as practicable and in accordance with the operation plan and annual tailings deposition plan (SRK 2015d and SRK 2020a).

This is done by pumping supernatant pond water from the East Lake TMF basin and discharging it into the Triangle Lake TMF (Figure 4). Accumulation of supernatant water would increase the seepage rates through the Expansion Dike and would need to be managed at the Back Pond and seepage collection ponds.

East Lake TMF Seepage Collection Ponds

Concrete Dams 1, 2, and 3 will serve as the primary containment for the East Lake TMF. These dams are situated downstream of the Expansion Dike and seepage through the structure will be collected at Seepage Ponds 1, 2, 3 and at the Back Pond (Figure 4). Water accumulating in the ponds will be discharged into the East Lake TMF basin where it will then be discharged to the Triangle Lake TMF.

The maximum operating level of the seepage ponds is 459.5 m elevation. There is minimal storage in the ponds and therefore pumps with float switches have been established to maintain the water levels below 459.5 m.

Triangle Lake TMF

The Triangle Lake TMF acts as the water repository for both TMFs, as water from the East Lake TMF is discharged to and managed at the Triangle Lake TMF. Supernatant water is either recirculated to the mill for processing or it is treated at the water treatment plant and subsequently discharged to the environment. Supernatant from the Triangle Lake TMF is pumped to the Back Pond where it is subsequently pumped to the Water Treatment Plant then to the Seabee Mill as reclaim.

The maximum operating level of the Triangle Lake TMF is 459.4 m during implementation of the 2020 Triangle Lake TMF deposition plan (SRK 2020c). The maximum operating level is forecasted to be 460.2 m during implementation of the 2021 Triangle Lake TMF deposition plan.

Triangle Lake Seepage Collection System

The Triangle Lake North Dam Seepage Collection System is located on the downstream toe of the North Dam and collects seepage from the TMF. Seepage water is continually pumped back to the Triangle Lake TMF by a float-operated pump and discharge line that extends from the sump to the TMF.

Back Pond

The Back Pond is located between the Expansion Dike and Concrete Dam 3 (Figure 4). Effluent from the Triangle Lake TMF is discharged into the Back Pond where it is either pumped to the East Lake water treatment plant for treatment or to the Seabee mill as reclaim water.

The maximum operating level of the Back Pond is 459.5 m elevation. The storage capacity of the Back Pond is approximately 70,500 m3.

East Pond

East Pond serves as a polishing pond for treated water from the Water Treatment Plant. Prior to entering the East Pond, water is sampled at Station East Lake moving bed bioreactor (MBBR) Discharge. Water from East Pond is discharged toward the airstrip and sampled at Station 1.5 final discharge point (FDP) prior to naturally flowing into Laonil Lake via Currie Rose Pond (Figure 4).

Water Treatment Process

During summer months under ice-free conditions, water from Triangle Lake TMF is treated at the Water Treatment Plant and discharged to the East Pond according to the following sequence:

1. Feed water undergoes a pre-treatment process.

2. Water is discharged into a clarifier pond (Pond 1).

3. Ammonia is reduced to nitrate via the moving bed bioreactor (MBBR).

4. Water is discharged into the holding pond (Pond 2).

5. Water is released to East Pond (Figure 4).

Water samples are collected from Station 1.5 (Figure 4) and analyzed for the parameters listed in Treatment and Discharge Parameters before water is discharged to the environment. If concentrations exceed the requirements summarized in Table 14, then water is pumped back to the East Lake TMF and retreated. Additional procedures on water quality sampling are provided in the Water Treatment Plant Operations Manual.
