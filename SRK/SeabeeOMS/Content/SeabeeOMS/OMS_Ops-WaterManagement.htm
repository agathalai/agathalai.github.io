Overview

In early 2022, a water balance for the TMFs was developed in GoldSim® to help designated Seabee environment and mill personnel manage the water in the TMFs through to closure (SRK 2022a). The GoldSim model can be updated and altered to include observations in the field or changes to the water management plan. The most recent update occurred in 2024 (2024-2025 Tailings Deposition Plan and Water Balance Update), and annual checks/updates will continue.

The water management process diagram should be read in conjunction with the following sections and is provided in Figure 2, which also shows active and backup pumps and water lines.

Planning and Principles

EL TMF is to be operated subaerially and any ponding must be pumped immediately to the TL TMF. Therefore, the pond levels defined below are specific to the TL TMF. The TL TMF Phase 1 and 2 basins will be operated separately until June 2027 when South Dam is breached, effectively combining Phase 1 and 2 basins.

Minimum Pond Levels

TL TMF Phase 1 Basin

  • the minimum operating pond volume in TL Phase 1 is 290,000 m3. This volume corresponds to six months of mill reclaim (160,000 m3) and allowance for 0.5 m of ice formation. The resulting pond elevation of 460.9 m will remain a fixed value until further deposition into the Phase 1 basin occurs in 2027.

TL TMF Phase 2 Basin

  • a minimum operating pond volume must be maintained in which there is at least 4 m of water cover in TL Phase 2 to prevent freezing of the reclaim in the winter months. The minimum pond volume and pond elevation will change over time as deposition advances and be updated annually in the OMS. The minimum operating pond volume for TL Phase 2 in 2024 is 40,000 m3, which results in a pond elevation of 457.5 m.

Maximum Operating Level

The maximum water elevation in TL TMF Phase 1 at which a 100 m exposed tailings beach is maintained at the North Dam. The Maximum Operating Level corresponds to a pond elevation of 462.4 m.

Full Supply Level

TL TMF Phase 1 Basin

  • the water level at which the minimum pond volume (290,000 m3) and the average combined freshet from both TMFs (189,900 m3) is stored. The fully supply level is a fixed elevation (461.9 m) based on storage of this total volume (464,900 m3) as the Phase 1 basin is at interim capacity (at capacity for the 463.0 m South Dam crest elevation).

TL TMF Phase 2 Basin

  • the water level at which the minimum pond volume (40,000 m3) and the average Phase 2 freshet (78,800 m3) is stored.

Maximum Water Level

  • the water level at which the minimum pond volume (290,000 m3), the average combined freshet from both TMFs, and the IDF from both TMFs is stored while maintain minimum freeboard for wave setup and runup (0.15 m) at the South Dam. The maximum water level is a fixed elevation (462.85 m) based on storage of this total volume (681,800 m3) when the Phase 1 basin is at interim capacity (at capacity for the 463.0 m South Dam crest elevation). The Phase 2 basin follows the same logic but has significantly more storage capacity; therefore, this condition is called the Max Operating Level (MOL). The MOL will change annually as tailings are placed in the Phase 2 basin and the water balance can be used to check this elevation.

Duration of Water Management

  • water treatment plant must be operated for as long as possible to reduce the water repository at the TL TMF (governed by the GoldSim water balance outputs and minimum pond volume).
  • two perimeter diversion sumps and pumps are to operate for as long as possible at the EL TMF to reduce inflow to the TMF.

Mill Water

Current water management needs at the Seabee mill for processing are supplied by the TMFs (95%) and from camp water and freshwater makeup (5%). An upgrade was completed in 2023 that allows for direct reclaim to the mill from the TL TMF. This upgrade was required as the Back Pond will be inundated with stabilization tailings (SRK 2022b).

Facilities

East Lake TMF Basin

Maximum allowable water levels in the EL TMF tailings basin must be maintained as low as practicable and in accordance with the operation plan and annual tailings deposition plan (SRK 2015d, SRK 2022a, and 2024-2025 Tailings Deposition Plan and Water Balance Update).

This is done by pumping supernatant pond water from the EL TMF basin and discharging it into the TL TMF (Figure 2). Accumulation of supernatant water would increase the seepage rates through the Expansion Dike and would need to be managed at the Back Pond and seepage collection ponds.

East Lake TMF Seepage Collection Ponds

Concrete Dams 1 and 2, and Dam 3 serve as the primary containment for the EL TMF. These dams are situated downstream of the Internal Rockfill Expansion Dike and the RTRB. Seepage through these structures are collected at the Ponds 1, 3, and 4 Wet Wells and at the Back Pond (Figure 2). Water accumulating in the ponds will be discharged into the EL TMF basin where it will then be discharged to the TL TMF.

The maximum operating level of the seepage ponds varies and is provided in the TARPs (Appendix B). There is minimal storage in the ponds and therefore pumps with float switches have been established to maintain the water levels at the MOL. Primary containment is provided by the concrete dams and bedrock highs, at elevation 460.0 m.

Back Pond

The Back Pond is located between the Expansion Dike and Concrete Dam 3 (Figure 3). The Back Pond is maintained as low as possible and water is discharged to the EL TMF basin.

The maximum operating level of the Back Pond is 463.2 m elevation. The storage capacity of the Back Pond is approximately 70,500 m3.

Construction of stabilization works for the EL TMF Expansion Dike began in 2022. Two new structures were constructed in the Back Pond area (one at the south end and one at the northeast corner). Tailings deposition into Back Pond began in May 2024. Therefore, beginning in 2024, the Back Pond area will no longer act as a mill reclaim lift station and water will be pumped directly from the TL TMF to the mill.

Triangle Lake TMF Phase 1

The TL TMF acts as the water repository for both TMFs, as water from the EL TMF is discharged to and managed at the TL TMF. Supernatant water is either recirculated to the mill for processing or it is treated at the water treatment plant and subsequently discharged to the environment.

Triangle Lake TMF Phase 2

Tailings will not be discharged into the TL TMF Phase 2 basin until December 2024 at the earliest. Therefore, any water that collects in the TL TMF Phase 2 basin until that time is considered non-contact water and acceptable for discharge to the environment. A diesel-powered dewatering pump is situated immediately upstream of Laonil Dam and will be operated during the open water season to keep water level as low as possible in the Phase 2 basin (Figure 2).

Triangle Lake Seepage Collection System

The TL North Dam Seepage Collection System is located on the downstream toe of the North Dam and collects seepage from the TMF. Seepage water is continually pumped back to the TL TMF by a float-operated pump and discharge line that extends from the sump to the TMF. Configuration of the sump and TARP Levels are provided in Appendix B.

East Pond

East Pond serves as a polishing pond for treated water from the Water Treatment Plant. Prior to entering the East Pond, water is sampled at Station EL moving bed bioreactor (MBBR) Discharge. Water from East Pond is discharged toward the airstrip and sampled at Station 1.5 final discharge point (FDP) prior to naturally flowing into Laonil Lake via Currie Rose Pond (Figure 2).

Water Treatment Process

During summer months under ice-free conditions, water from TL TMF is treated at the Water Treatment Plant and discharged to the East Pond according to the following sequence:

  1. feed water undergoes a pre-treatment process.
  2. water is discharged into a clarifier pond (Pond 1).
  3. ammonia is reduced to nitrate via the moving bed bioreactor (MBBR).
  4. water is discharged into the holding pond (Pond 2).
  5. water is released to East Pond (Figure 2).

Water samples are collected from Station 1.5 at the north end of East Pond (Figure 2) and analyzed for the parameters listed in Treatment and Discharge Parameters before water is discharged to the environment. If concentrations exceed the requirements summarized in Section 9.3.4, then water is pumped back to the EL TMF and retreated. Additional procedures on water quality sampling are provided in the Water Treatment Plant Operations Manual.
