Objectives

The objectives of the surveillance program are to

  • regularly monitor the operational performance of the TMFs and their components,
  • regularly review and interpret surveillance data, and
  • report observations in consistent formats.

Surveillance information is gathered through visual inspections, monitoring performance, safety audits, dam safety reviews, and data collection. Ongoing review of qualitative and quantitative surveillance information informs appropriate preventative maintenance.

Scope

Surveillance goals are achieved by implementing three main elements: inspections, collection of monitoring data, and documentation and management of surveillance data. Each of these are addressed in the topics under this section.

Responsibility

The Site Responsible Person is responsible for ensuring the completion of monitoring, data collection, and physical inspection of the TMF. See Roles and Responsibilities for additional details.

The following personnel operates and perform surveillance for various aspects of the TMFs:

  • Site Responsible Person and environmental personnel
  • Site services superintendent and site services personnel
  • Site survey personnel
  • Mill superintendent or mill operating personnel
  • Water treatment plant personnel
  • Mechanical foreman or mechanical personnel
  • Electrical foreman or electrical personnel

All personnel involved in any aspect of the TMFs operation must be aware of and understand their responsibilities with respect to the implementation of this manual.

Documentation

To ensure successful implementation of this manual, it is crucial that all surveillance components and data are documented, managed, and reported in an efficient and logical manner. Necessary documentation for all phases of surveillance is described in the relevant subsections.

The following documents contain figures and charts needed for surveillance activities:

  • Annual tailings deposition figures. These figures are to be printed and used during inspections. These figures indicate the tailings deposition sequence, tailings deposition surface, and pond location.
  • Appendix A: Inspection Forms. These forms are to be printed and filled out during all visual inspections.
  • Appendix B: Trigger Action Response Plans for Monitoring Data.

Reporting

The surveillance and inspection reports should be submitted to the EOR on a regular basis. After being reviewed, digital records of all surveillance and inspection activities should be kept on file for future reference.

Levels of Inspection

Successful implementation of a surveillance program requires inspections at a scheduled frequency that aligns with the facilities' requirements. Surveillance is based on four levels of inspection:

  • Routine daily inspections
  • Weekly comprehensive inspections
  • Annual geotechnical inspections
  • Dam safety reviews

Visual Aids

Visual aids must be established on site to facilitate inspections of the TMFs, such as these listed below:

  • Markers placed along the upstream crest of structures indicating maximum tailings deposition and maximum operating levels.
  • Markers placed within deposited tailings indicating required exposed beach length (50 and 100 m).
  • Staff gauges installed in both TMFs, the seepage collection ponds, and Back Pond at the East Lake TMF.
