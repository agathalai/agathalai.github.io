Settlement and Instability of Dams or Dikes

  • The following tables provide reporting, immediate actions, and detailed instructions for instability conditions at dams or dikes.
  • Corrective Action Conditions (Orange) are associated with the EPP.
  • Critical Condition (Red) area associated with the ERP.

Green

Acceptable Condition

Reporting

None

Action

Continue operation, maintenance, and surveillance guidelines as specified in corresponding sections of this manual.

Yellow

Early Warning Condition

Reporting

Implement EPRP reporting procedure in (Early Warning â€“ Appendix D).

Immediate Actions

Increase frequency of monitoring, surveillance, and data collection as per the TARP (Appendix B).

Orange

Corrective Action Condition

Reporting

Implement TARP Reporting Procedure (Corrective Action - Appendix D).

Immediate Actions
  • Gather data.
  • Engage EOR.

Detailed Instructions
  • Perform hazard assessment before entering the area.
  • Take photographs.
  • Record the location of the observation with GPS.
  • Record the size of the feature (use a logbook or object in the photo for reference).
  • Any damage to the liner should be recorded with GPS, clearly identified with spray paint or equivalent, and photographed.
  • Engage EOR to develop any remedial measures that can be implemented.

Red

Critical Condition

Reporting

Implement TARP Reporting Procedure (Critical Condition - Appendix D).

Immediate Actions
  • Block off and restrict access to the affected area.
  • Focus all efforts on outreach, planning, and preparation until the failure event has concluded and conditions at the area of influence are stable.
  • Implement associated tasks (as applicable) once it is safe to do so.

Detailed Instructions
  • Perform hazard assessment before entering the area.
  • Stop tailings deposition into the TMF.
  • Construct a tailings containment berm downstream of the area of influence, if possible.
  • Stabilize the damaged embankment, if possible.
  • If possible, lower any remaining supernatant ponding in the facility according to the sequence of tasks outlined in the EPP.
  • Arrange immediate site visit with the EOR.
  • If Laonil Lake is impacted: distribute notice to stop use of freshwater in camp.
  • Repair any structural damage in accordance with EOR recommendations.
  • Conduct comprehensive study of the impact of the release on the downstream environment/waterbody.
  • Based on the conclusions of all post-impact studies, work with the EOR to develop plan for remedial action.
