The Phase 2 expansion to achieve the final configuration of the TMF is complete (SRK 2022c). The current configuration of the TL TMF is shown in Figures 2 and 3. The history of dam construction is summarized below.

Year

Milestone

2019
  • A portion of the Quarry and Laonil Dam foundations were exposed. Structural bedrock mapping was completed beneath the highest portion of Laonil Dam to assess requirement for foundation improvements (grouting, etc).

2020
  • Construction of Portage South, Portage West, Quarry, and Laonil Dams was advanced.

  • Concrete plinth was constructed at Quarry Dam but no HDPE liner was installed at any of the dams.

2021

  • Construction of Portage South, Portage West, Quarry, and Laonil Dams was complete to 466.0 m.

Triangle Lake TMF: Portage SOUTH Dam

The Portage South Dam is located on the east edge of the Phase 2 TL TMF between Portage North Dam and Portage West Dam. The construction of Portage South Dam is summarized in the table below.

Key design characteristics of the Portage South Dam are as follows:

Parameter

Portage South Dam

Foundation

Bedrock

Construction

  • Crushed rock (bedding material and transition material) layers on top of bulk dam fill. Bulk dam fill consists of blasted run of quarry rock (SRK 2022c)

  • The low permeable component of the dam consists of 80 mil thick HDPE geomembrane attached to a reinforced concrete plinth along the upstream toe

  • The geomembrane extends to elevation 466.0 m (current and final permitted height of the structure)

Triangle Lake TMF: Laonil Dam

The Laonil Dam is located at the southwest corner of the Phase 2 TL TMF between Portage West Dam and Quarry Dam. The construction of Laonil Dam is summarized in the table below.

Key design characteristics of the Laonil Dam are as follows:

Parameter

Laonil Dam

Foundation

Bedrock

Construction

  • Crushed rock (bedding material and transition material) layers on top of bulk dam fill. Bulk dam fill consists of blasted run of quarry rock (SRK 2022c).

  • The low permeable component of the dam consists of 80 mil thick HDPE geomembrane attached to a reinforced concrete plinth along the upstream toe.

  • HDPE liner is contained between bedding layers from the foundation to elevation 459.0 m and installed above bedding layers from 459.0 m to 466.0 m (i.e., zig zag liner with the liner being internal to the dam below 459.0 m).

  • The geomembrane extends to elevation 466.0 m (current and final permitted height of the structure).

Approximate Length

200 m

Approximate Width

10 m

Maximum Height

Approximately 17.5 m at the deepest section of the dam

Upstream slopes

2.3H:1V to 2.9H:1V

Downstream slope

1.5H:1V
