Dam Safety Review (DSR)

Objectives

The purpose of the DSR is to

  • assess the containment facilities to validate that the day-to-day operations and engineering practices conform to industry good practice,
  • ensure that all legal and/or regulatory requirements are met,
  • review the hazard identification and assessment, and
  • review any changes in dam/dike classification in accordance with the CDA safety guidelines.

Scope

The DSR consists of an independent systematic assessment of all aspects of design, construction, maintenance, operation, processes, and systems affecting the safety of the facility.

The review should encompass all elements of the dam/dike systems and be based on current knowledge and industry standards, which might be different from those in effect at the time of construction or of prior safety reviews.

The most recent DSR was completed in 2017 (SRK 2018c) and the next review is scheduled for 2024.

Frequency

The DSR should be carried out once every seven years (as per the most recent CDA guideline).

Responsible Persons

This inspection must be completed by qualified geotechnical engineers.

Reporting

The results of the DSR are presented in a factual report that includes findings, recommendations, and mitigation actions where required.

Guidance

A DSR is to be completed in accordance with current CDA guidelines (CDA 2014).
