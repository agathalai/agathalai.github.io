Overview

Updated tailings deposition plan(s) are completed on an annual basis. The most recent deposition plan for the EL and TL TMFs was completed in 2024 (2024-2025 Tailings Deposition Plan and Water Balance Update).

Planning and Principles

Tailings Discharge

  • tailings are discharged subaerially through perimeter discharge locations.
  • discharge locations and durations are designed to create Tailings Beaches in accordance with the annual tailings deposition planning.
  • following completion of the stabilization construction at the EL TMF in 2022, tailings deposition will periodically be re-instated into the EL TMF. Detailed sequencing will be captured and documented in the annual tailings deposition planning.
  • as with previous operations, tailings will only be deposited into the EL TMF in the summer months (typically May to October). Note, final tailings deposition in the EL TMF as part of the stabilization design will be completed in 2025.

Tailings Beaches

  • exposed beaches of tailings are maintained on the upstream face of all perimeter containment structures for both TMFs. At the TL TMF North Dam, the tailings beach must be at least 100 m in length.
  • following completion of the Phase 2 TL TMF expansion in 2021 (SRK 2022c), the South Dam is now internal to the overall TL TMF and therefore is no longer anticipated or required to have an exposed tailings beach.
  • tailings beaches reduce seepage rates through containment structures by increasing upstream seepage paths and lowering hydraulic gradient.

Ponded Water

Ponded water in both TMFs will be directed to the reclaim stations established on their western shorelines.

Final Landform

At closure, discharge locations will form a final tailings landform that will:

  • permit placement of a dust cover; and
  • create positive drainage toward a final discharge location such as a closure spillway.

Summary of Most Recent Deposition Planning

The life of mine (LOM) deposition plans for the EL TMF and TL TMF are provided in 2024-2025 Tailings Deposition Plan and Water Balance Update. Detailed annual tailings deposition plans will continue and will supersede this plan.

East Lake TMF

Due to upstream construction of the EL TMF Expansion Dike and related stability concerns, tailings have not been deposited in the TMF since November 2019. Taking into consideration construction of the stabilization works at the EL TMF in 2022, the next period of tailings deposition in this facility commenced in the summer months of 2024 following the tailings deposition plan in (SRK 2022b). A 2024-2025 Seabee Tailings Deposition Plan and Water Balance Update was completed (Appendix E), which includes the details for the EL TMF Back Pond and EL TMF basin. The final deposition period in the EL TMF will include approximately five months of deposition in the Main Basin to establish the closure configuration. Timing of the final five months of deposition is unknown at this time.

Triangle Lake TMF

Deposition summary for the TL TMF between November 2024 to ~July 2027 is summarized in the 2024-2025 Seabee Tailings Deposition Plan and Water Balance Update (Appendix E). This memorandum also includes the long-term tailings deposition plan, which will be checked annually. Tailings deposition will move back to the EL TMF in May 2025 and will remain in this basin until the stabilization configuration is complete (~end of August).

Monitoring Procedure

The table below summarizes required monitoring and the frequency of inspections for all tailings deposition parameters.

Inspection

Frequency

Critical Observations

Exposed Pipeline
  • Daily
  • Prior to starting tailings pumps
  • After shutting down of tailings pumps
  • Any time the flow is switched between pipes
  • Tailings discharge rates
  • Damage to exposed pipelines
  • Signs of leaking, cracking, and ice/tailings blockages

Discharge Elevation

Weekly, or more frequently at the discretion of the engineer
  • Elevations of tailings discharge
  • Cumulative deposition volume of tailings

Tailings Beach

Weekly, or more frequently at the discretion of the engineer
  • Tailings beach slope and distance from discharge location
  • Aerial photographs

Bathymetric Survey of Each Pond

Annually, or more frequently at the discretion of the engineer
  • Depth and lateral extent of subaqueous tailings surface
  • Slope of subaqueous tailings surface

Note: Specific thresholds requiring action and their corresponding Trigger Action Response Plans (TARPs) are provided in Overview, and Trigger Action Response Plans (TARPs) for Monitoring Data. The configuration of the deposited tailings surface for both TMFs is provided annually and must be followed to facilitate surveillance of the TMFs.
