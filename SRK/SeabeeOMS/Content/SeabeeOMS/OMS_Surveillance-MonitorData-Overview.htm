Objectives

Monitoring and the efficient and logical documentation, management, and review of all monitoring data is essential to the successful implementation of this manual.

Scope

Regular monitoring and data collection allow for appropriate surveillance of the TMF components. The various requirements are categorized by the type and frequency of monitoring and data collection.

Frequency

Monitoring data is to be collected at a predetermined frequency that allows appropriate surveillance of the TMF components. This frequency may change depending upon the data and their relation to the trigger action response plans (TARPs), which are summarized in Appendix B.

All data must be reviewed by appropriate qualified staff immediately following collection to confirm integrity of the instrumentation, as well as to ensure that the dam/dike performance is consistent with expectations.

The surveillance and inspection reports should be submitted to the EOR on a regular basis.

Responsible Persons

The surveillance and inspection reports should be prepared by a competent person under the supervision of the Site Responsible Person and be reviewed by the General Manager.

The EOR should:

  • receive surveillance and inspection reports on a regular basis;
  • be kept informed of any changes to the monitoring protocol; and
  • be contracted when needed to validate the integrity of the instrumentation and the performance of the dams and dikes (if local personnel cannot).

Documentation

The surveillance and inspection reports should adhere to the following guidelines:

  • All data must be reviewed by appropriate qualified staff immediately following collection to confirm integrity of the instrumentation, as well as to ensure that the dam/dike performance is consistent with expectations.
  • If the staff are not comfortable drawing such conclusions, the EOR must be contracted to perform these duties.
  • After being reviewed, records of all surveillance, inspection, and monitoring activities should be kept on file in electronic format for future reference.
  • Hard-copy notes must be scanned, and the raw data saved together with any transposed data.

Any changes to the monitoring made on site should be immediately conveyed to the EOR as to not create confusion during data review. For example, if the stickup of a monitoring well is altered (raised/lowered), this data must be conveyed to all pertinent parties so that precise data review is not compromised.

Reporting

The surveillance reports should be submitted to the EOR on a regular basis.
