East Lake TMF Internal Rockfill Expansion Dike

Carry out the following surveillance activities and actions, as appropriate.

Inspection

  • read staff gauge within the reclaim shack that transfers water to the TL TMF Phase 1 basin and obtain reading of pond level in masl.
  • refer and compare field data to TARPs (Appendix B).
  • pond level to be managed as low as practicable via discharge to TL TMF.
  • record pond level and monitor pond volume.
  • water level must be as low as practical or at a maximum of the ‘Early Warning’ TARP (i.e., below elevation 461.8 m).

Deficiencies and Corrective Action

  • contact the Site Responsible Person and the EOR.
  • refer and compare field data to TARPs (Appendix B).

Critical Observations

The pond level is at or above the maximum allowable operating level as described in most recent tailings deposition plan and TARPs “i.e. the red critical condition” (Appendix B).

Back Pond

Carry out the following surveillance activities and actions, as appropriate.

Inspection

  • read staff gauge at the pump transfer station from Back Pond to the EL TMF basin and obtain reading of pond level in masl.
  • pond level should be within the Acceptable Operating Conditions (i.e., below Elev. 462.1 m).
  • at a maximum, pond level must be at or below the tailings elevation along the RTRB. This will change over time and SGO must periodically survey the tailings beach. At the end of the 2024 operating season it is estimated that this tailings elevations will be at approximately 462.5 m but that must be confirmed.

Deficiencies and Corrective Action

  • contact the Site Responsible Person and EOR.
  • refer and compare field data to TARPS (Appendix B).

East Lake TMF Seepage Ponds

Carry out the following surveillance activities and actions, as appropriate.

Inspection

  • Read staff gauge SG1, SG2, and SG3 and obtain reading of pond level in masl.
  • Pond level should be within the Acceptable Operating Conditions (i.e., Green Acceptable Operating Condition, Appendix B).
  • At a maximum, pond level must be at or below 0.2 m from freeboard freeboard (i.e., below Elev. 459.8 m, the Red – Critical Condition, Appendix B).

Deficiencies and Corrective Action

  • Contact the Site Responsible Person and the EOR.
  • Refer and compare field data to TARPS (Appendix B) and the Emergency Preparedness and Response Plan.

Triangle Lake TMF

Carry out the following surveillance activities and actions, as appropriate.

Inspection

  • Read staff gauge in the TL TMF Reclaim Shack and obtain reading of pond level in masl.
  • Pond level to be managed at the target operating levels, refer to current tailings deposition plan and Water Balance.
  • If possible, pond level should be operated at a level that will permit exposed tailings beaches along the containment structures (specifically the North Dam).
  • Water level must be within the Acceptable Operating Conditions (i.e., below the maximum operating level).

Deficiencies and Corrective Action

  • Contact the Site Responsible Person and the EOR.
  • Refer and compare field data to TARPS (Appendix B) and the Emergency Preparedness and Response Plan.
