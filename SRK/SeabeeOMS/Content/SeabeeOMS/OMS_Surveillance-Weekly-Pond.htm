East Lake TMF Expansion Dike

Carry out the following surveillance activities and actions, as appropriate.

Inspection

  • Read staff gauge SG5 and obtain reading of pond level in masl.
  • Pond level to be managed as low as practicable via discharge to Triangle Lake TMF.
  • Record pond level and monitor pond volume.
  • Pond volume to be maintained at a level that is not greater than the available storage capacity in the Back Pond at any given time.
  • Water level must be at or below 0.5 m freeboard (i.e., below elevation 459.5 m).

Deficiencies

  • Contact the Site Responsible Person and the EOR.
  • Refer and compare field data to TARPs (Appendix B).

Critical Observations

The pond level is at or above the maximum allowable operating level as described in most recent tailings deposition plan and TARPs (Appendix B).

Actions Following Critical Observations

  • Implement the Emergency Preparedness and Response Plan.
  • As soon as practicably possible, contact the Site Responsible Person and the EOR.
  • Check for seepage along the downstream toe of the structures.
  • Check if seepage rates to the seepage collection ponds are above design values (22.5 L/s).
  • For a pond level past the allowable limit, take the following actions:
    • Check that the discharge line to Triangle Lake TMF is working properly. If not use backup pumps (Figure 4).
    • Pump excess water to Triangle Lake TMF. If this cannot be done, pump excess water to the Back Pond.
    • Start the Water Treatment Plant (if not already running) and the evaporators.
    • Decrease or stop tailings deposition.
    • Decrease freshwater usage in the camp.
  • At last resort, discharge to the environment. This would be done in a controlled manner but would be a release of supernatant to the environment.

Back Pond

Carry out the following surveillance activities and actions, as appropriate.

Inspection

  • Read staff gauge SG4 and obtain reading of pond level in masl.
  • Pond level must be at or below 0.5 m from freeboard (i.e., below Elev. 459.5 m).

Deficiencies

  • Contact the Site Responsible Person and EOR.
  • Refer and compare field data to TARPS (Appendix B).

Critical Observations

The pond level is at or above the maximum allowable operating level as described in most recent tailings deposition plan and TARPs (Appendix B).

Actions Following Critical Observations

  • Implement the Emergency Preparedness and Response Plan.
  • As soon as practicably possible, contact the Site Responsible Person and the EOR.
  • Check for seepage along the downstream of Concrete Dam 3 and if seepage rates to the seepage collection ponds are above design values (22.5 L/s).
  • For a pond level past the allowable limit, apply the following:
    • Discharge excess water into the East Lake TMF basin, which is then pumped to the Triangle Lake TMF.
    • Stop the transfer pump from Triangle Lake TMF to the Back Pond so that reclaim can draw down the Back Pond.
    • Reverse the pumps so that water from the Back Pond is discharged to the Triangle Lake TMF.
    • Start the Water Treatment Plant (if not already running) and the evaporators.
    • Decrease or stop tailings deposition in the East Lake or Triangle Lake TMF.
    • Decrease freshwater usage in the camp, which may increase the amount of reclaim to the mill.
  • At last resort, discharge to the environment. This would be done in a controlled manner but would be a release of supernatant to the environment.

East Lake TMF Seepage Ponds

Carry out the following surveillance activities and actions, as appropriate.

Inspection

  • The pond level must be at or below 0.5 m from freeboard (i.e., below Elev. 459.5 m).

Deficiencies

  • Contact the Site Responsible Person and the EOR.
  • Refer and compare field data to TARPS (Appendix B) and the Emergency Preparedness and Response Plan.

Critical Observations

The pond level is at or above the maximum allowable operating level as described in most recent tailings deposition plan and TARPs (Appendix B).

Actions Following Critical Observations

  • Implement the Emergency Preparedness and Response Plan.
  • As soon as practicably possible, contact the Site Responsible Person and the EOR.
  • Check for seepage along the downstream of Concrete Dams 1, 2A, 2B, and 2C.
  • For a pond level past the allowable limit, apply the following:
    • Discharge excess water into the East Lake TMF basin, which is then pumped to the Triangle Lake TMF.
    • Discharge excess water to the Back Pond, if Step 1 cannot be done:
    • Stop the transfer pump from Triangle Lake TMF to the Back Pond so that reclaim can draw down the Back Pond.
    • Start the Water Treatment Plant (if not already running) and the evaporators.
    • Decrease or stop tailings deposition in the East Lake TMF.
    • Decrease freshwater usage in the camp, which may increase the amount of reclaim to the mill.
  • At last resort, discharge to the environment. This would be done in a controlled manner but would be a release of supernatant to the environment.

Triangle Lake TMF

Carry out the following surveillance activities and actions, as appropriate.

Inspection

  • Read staff gauge SG6 and obtain reading of pond level in masl.
  • Pond level to be managed at the target operating levels, refer to current tailings deposition plan and Water Balance.
  • If possible, pond level should be operated at a level that will permit exposed tailings beaches along the containment structures.
  • Water level must be at or below the maximum operating level (i.e., below Elev. 459.4).

Deficiencies

  • Contact the Environmental Superintendent and the EOR.
  • Refer and compare field data to TARPS (Appendix B) and the Emergency Preparedness and Response Plan.

Critical Observations

The pond level is at or above the full supply level (FSL, Elev. 462.5 m) as described in the most recent tailings deposition plan and TARPs (Appendix B).

Actions Following Critical Observations

  • Implement the Emergency Preparedness and Response Plan.
  • As soon as practicably possible, contact the Environmental Superintendent and the EOR.
  • Check for seepage along the downstream of the South Dam, North Dam and Saddle dikes.
  • For a pond level past the allowable limit in the Triangle Lake TMF, apply the following as applicable:
    • Discharge as much water as possible to the Back Pond.
    • Start the Water Treatment Plant (if not already running and if it can be run) and the evaporators.
    • Decrease or stop tailings deposition in the East Lake or Triangle Lake TMFs.
    • Decrease freshwater usage in the camp, which may increase the amount of reclaim to the mill.
  • At last resort, discharge to the environment in the vicinity of the South Dam Emergency Spillway. This would be done in a controlled manner but would be a release of supernatant to the environment.
