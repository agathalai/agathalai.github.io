Objectives

Water quality in East Pond is monitored for its suitability for discharge to the environment.

Scope and Frequency

The table below lists the critical observations relative to East Pond (Station 1.5) monitoring.

Frequency

Observation

Weekly
  • pH
  • temperature
  • conductivity
  • total suspended solids
  • major ions
  • ammonia
  • As
  • Cu
  • Pd
  • Ni
  • Zn
  • total cyanide

Monthly
  • Toxicity
  • Radium-226

 

Note: Weekly samplings are to coincide on the same dates as monthly acute toxicity sampling.

Responsible Persons

Environmental personnel are responsible for water quality monitoring at East Pond (Station 1.5).

Documentation / Data Management

  • Water samples are to be collected following the appropriate industry-accepted standards.
  • The following records should be logged in the environment personnel’s field logbook: date and time, sample ID, and sample location.
  • Water samples must be correctly labelled for identification and transport.
  • Once laboratory results are received, the file must be placed in the appropriate location on the Seabee Mine server for direct upload to the Seabee Mine Environmental Database.
  • Quality control should be applied to the data by ensuring that the file is complete and matches the file format for the direct upload procedure.

Trigger Action Response Plans (TARPs)

East Pond water quality does not have defined TARP. Data is reviewed by SGO Environment and must meet the Schedule 1 water quality limits in Treatment and Discharge Parameters.
