Self-Learning on Video Analytics Devices

Self-learning is the ability of a video analytics appliance or camera to perform self-adjustment of the scene. When self-learning is enabled, the video analytics device adjusts itself to the activity in its field of view. This can significantly improve the accuracy of human and vehicle classification.

Enable self-learning in the Settings dialog box. Self Learning The Progress: status in the dialog box tells you the following information:

  • 0% - 33% — The device is in the initial learning stage where it begins to gather information on the scene.
  • 34% - 66% — The device is adjusting itself using the data it has gathered on the average person and average vehicle in the scene.
  • 67% - 100% — The device has established a high level of classified object detection accuracy.

The time needed for self-learning to reach 100% depends on the amount of activity in the scene. It generally requires approximately 200 high-confidence detections through the camera field of view for optimal self-learning calibration.

Once the camera becomes stable after initial installation, it is strongly recommended that the camera's self-learning setting be reset. During installation, a camera is frequently adjusted, so any self-learning the system was able to do would become invalid.

NOTE: Always reset self-learning after a camera is physically moved or adjusted, and if the focus or zoom level is changed. The change in the camera's field of view affects the video analytic results.

When self-learning is reset, all previous self-learning data is deleted and the device learns anew. By allowing the video analytics device to re-learn the scene, you are able to prevent false detections based on old data.

Typically, you only need to enable self-learning for the video analytics device to learn the scene. However, if the device continues to produce a large number of false object classifications after self-learning is complete, you may want to use the Teach By Example feature to refine the device's object classification capabilities. For more information, see Teach By Example

It is highly recommended that self-learning be enabled for all video analytics devices, except in the following circumstances:

  • If you do not expect any humans or vehicles in the device's field of view.
  • Scenes where objects can be observed to be moving at different heights. For example, overhead pedestrian bridges, train platforms, hills and underpasses.
